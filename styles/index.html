The most common words or phrases used in job titles

The word cloud visualization is generated by extracting job titles from a DataFrame and representing them in a graphical format. The size of each word in the cloud corresponds to its frequency or significance relative to other words in the dataset. Analysis of the word cloud reveals that roles related to ownership, operation, and directorship constitute a substantial portion of the job market, underscoring the importance of leadership skill development. Additionally, sales, programming, and engineering skills emerge as commonly sought-after attributes. It's important to note that the choice of colors in the visualization is purely for aesthetic appeal and does not convey any specific meaning related to the textual content.

Top 3 Industries Sankey Diagram

Interactive Sankey Diagram created using D3, along with Javascript, CSS, and HTML. The Javascript is where most of the code is, where the CSS was used to increase the size of the page to display more information. The HTML then implemented the JS and CSS. Keywords were found in each job to separate the industries into three categories: Business (Orange), Healthcare (Green) and Technology (Cyan). To see the data more clearly you can drag the nodes on the right around. Additionally, you can hover over them to see their industry and how many of those position titles are listed. We can see from the data that the majority of job postings are in the business sector currently, which explains why the technology and healthcare industries can be so competitive as there are less available jobs. The interactivity of the Sankey Diagram adds depth to the visualization, allowing users to engage with the data dynamically. By dragging nodes and hovering over them, users can gain insights into the distribution of job postings across different industries. This insight into the prevalence of business sector positions sheds light on the nature of the technology and healthcare fields, where opportunities may be comparatively scarce.

Heatmap of Top Skill Names by Experience Level

The heatmap visualizes the top 10 most sought-after skills, ranked by the number of job postings, with the X-axis representing skill names and the Y-axis representing the experience levels. The intensity of red shading indicates the frequency of job postings on LinkedIn. The graph reveals that entry-level and mid-senior roles dominate the job market across skill categories. Particularly, Information Technology and engineering stand out as high-demand sectors for mid-senior level positions. However, the scarcity of internship opportunities amidst the job postings presents a concerning trend for college students. This pattern underscores a systemic issue where companies prioritize hiring individuals with existing skills, neglecting investment in internship programs. This dearth of internships poses a significant challenge for college students aiming to gain practical experience and advance their careers.
